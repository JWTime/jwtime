FAQ

Quick answers about privacy, content, cache and connections

Project Status

Is JW Time an official app?

No. JW Time is an independent, free, non-commercial project created by volunteers. It is not affiliated with, sponsored by, or officially approved by any organization.

Does the name JW Time mean the app is official?

No. The name describes the intended use of the app: helping with timing and technical support. It is not meant to imply official approval or representation by any organization.

Is JW Time commercial?

No. JW Time is distributed free of charge and has no advertising, in-app purchases or paid features.

Content and Media

Does JW Time follow official guidance?

JW Time is designed for local, personal, free and non-commercial use, respecting official guidance about the use of content. The app does not include official content in the installer and does not redistribute it through its own servers.

Does JW Time perform live scraping of official websites?

No. JW Time does not crawl or copy web pages in real time. For programs and media, it uses public files and official distribution channels; downloaded content is prepared and used locally on the user’s computer.

How are media files retrieved?

When you use the Media window, JW Time can request files through publicly available official channels and save them in the local cache. You can also add local files that are already on the computer.

Privacy and Connections

Does JW Time collect personal data or telemetry?

No. JW Time does not automatically collect or send personal data, telemetry, usage reports, crash reports or logs to the developer. Settings, profiles, programs, recordings, logs and cache remain on the user’s computer.

Does the connection test send data to the developer?

No. The connection test shows the result inside the app. You can copy it to the clipboard and share it manually only if you want assistance.

What Internet connections can JW Time make?

JW Time can connect to official sources for programs and media, to the JW Time site for documentation, communications and support files, and to external services only for explicit features such as network diagnostics or temporary remote access if enabled by the user.

Local Files and Cache

Do downloaded files stay forever?

No. Downloaded files are used as local cache or offline packages. Some temporary or old files are cleaned automatically where supported; you can also clear cache, packages and offline media from the app settings. Local files added manually remain where the user chose to keep them.

Can I use JW Time offline?

Yes, for content that has already been downloaded, imported or added manually. The first download and content updates require an Internet connection.
