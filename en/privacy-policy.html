🔒 Privacy Policy

How JW Time manages and protects your data

Last updated: June 3, 2025

1. Introduction

JW Time manages and displays timers during meetings or assemblies, synchronizing data across local networks while maintaining privacy compliance with GDPR standards where applicable.

2. Personal Data Collection

User-Provided Data

The application does not require the entry of personal information such as name, email address, phone number, or other identifying information.

IP Addresses and Technical Data

The app may log the IP addresses of devices on the local network for debugging purposes. Under GDPR, IP addresses may constitute personal data when linked to other identifying information.

However, JW Time logs this data only locally and does not transmit or share it with third-party servers.

Integrated Web Server

A local web server displays timer status to devices on the network. Data transmission occurs only within the LAN, limited to part titles and remaining time.

No user personal data is collected, stored, or transmitted.

3. Purpose and Legal Basis

Data processing is based on legitimate interest under Article 6(1)(f) of GDPR for:

  • Debugging and diagnostic functions: Resolving technical issues and improving the app
  • Local network security monitoring: Verifying local network connections
  • App functionality maintenance: Ensuring proper operation

4. Data Sharing and Transfer

JW Time DOES NOT send information to external services, cloud servers, or third parties.

All timer data and logs remain on the user's device or local network. There is no data transfer to the Internet.

5. Data Security and Retention

Logs are stored locally on the user's device (for example, in the %APPDATA%\JWTimeLogs folder on Windows).

Data remains stored until the user manually deletes it. There is no remote storage or automatic backup to external servers.

How to Delete Data

  • Access the log folder on your device
  • Delete the files manually
  • Or completely uninstall the application

6. User Rights under GDPR

If you reside in the European Union, you have the following rights:

📋 Access

Right to access data logged locally on your device

✏️ Rectification

Right to correct or update stored data

🗑️ Erasure

Right to delete logs and stored data at any time

⛔ Restriction

Right to restrict the processing of your data

🚫 Objection

Right to object to the processing of your data

⚖️ Complaint

Right to lodge a complaint with data protection authorities

7. How to Exercise Your Rights

Since all data is stored locally on your device, you can exercise your rights directly:

  • Access: Navigate to the log folder on your device
  • Erasure: Delete log files from the %APPDATA%\JWTimeLogs folder
  • Restriction: Disable logging features in the app settings (if available)

For further assistance, you can contact us.

8. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated periodically to reflect technical or regulatory developments. Users are encouraged to review this page regularly.

The date of the last update is indicated at the beginning of this document.
