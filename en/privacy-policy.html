🔒 Privacy Policy

How JW Time manages and protects your data

Last update: 3 June 2025

1. Introduction

JW Time manages and displays timers during meetings or assemblies, synchronizing data across local networks while maintaining privacy compliance to standards GDPR where applicable.

2. Collection of Personal Data

Data Provided by the User

The application does not require the entry of personal information such as name, email address, telephone number or other information identifiers.

IP Addresses and Technical Data

The app can record the IP addresses of devices on the local network for purposes debugging. According to the GDPR, IP addresses can constitute personal data when linked to other identifying information.

However, JW Time only records this data locally and does not transmit it nor does it share them with third-party servers.

Integrated Web Server

A local web server displays the status of timers to devices on the network. The Data transmission occurs only within the LAN, limited to titles of the parts and the remaining time.

No personal data about you is collected, stored or transmitted.

3. Purpose and Legal Basis

Data processing is based on legitimate interest pursuant to Article 6(1)(f) of the GDPR for:

  • Debugging and diagnostic functions: Resolve technical problems and improve the app
  • Monitor the security of the local network: Check connections to the local network
  • Maintain the functionality of the app: Ensure correct functioning

4. Data Sharing and Transfer

JW Time DOES NOT send information to external services, cloud servers or third parties.

All timer data and logs remain on the user's device or local network. There is no data transfer to the Internet.

5. Security and Data Storage

Logs are stored locally on the user's device (for example, in the %APPDATA%\JWTimeLogs folder on Windows).

The data remains stored until the user manually deletes it. There is no remote archive or automatic backup on external servers.

How to Delete Data

  • Access the logs folder on your device
  • Delete files manually
  • Or completely uninstall the application

6. User Rights under GDPR

If you are a resident of the European Union, you have the following rights:

📋 Access

Right to access data recorded locally on your device

✏️ Rectification

Right to correct or update stored data

🗑️ Deletion

Right to delete logs and stored data at any time

⛔ Limitation

Right to limit the processing of your data

🚫 Object

Right to object to the processing of your data

⚖️ Complaint

Right to lodge a complaint with the data protection authorities

7. How to Exercise Your Rights

Since all data is stored locally on your device, you can exercise your rights directly:

  • Access: Navigate to the logs folder on your device
  • Deletion: Delete log files from the folder %APPDATA%\JWTimeLogs
  • Restriction: Disable logging features in the app settings (if available)

For further assistance, you can contact us.

8. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated periodically to reflect developments technical or regulatory. Users are encouraged to consult this page regularly.

The date of the last update is indicated at the beginning of this document.
